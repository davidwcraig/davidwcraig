A bit of math

The flow of heat through a medium can be described by a flux vector `vec F`, whose direction gives the direction of the heat flow and whose magnitude gives the magnitude of the heat flow in cal / cmÂ² / sec. This vector `vec F` is related to the gradient of the temperature `T` by the thermal conductivity `K` of the medium:

`vec F = - K grad T.`

We also introduce the specific heat `c` and the density `rho` of the medium.

In terms of these quantities we can write two different but equal expressions for the rate of change with time `t` of the heat `Q` in a volume `V`:

`(dQ)/(dt) = int_V d^3 x c rho (del T)/(del t) = - int_S d vec A * vec F`
